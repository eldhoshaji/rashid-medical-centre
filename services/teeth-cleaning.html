Why Regular Dental Cleaning Matters

Even with diligent brushing and flossing at home, plaque and tartar gradually accumulate in areas that your toothbrush simply cannot reach. Over time, this buildup hardens into calculus, a stubborn deposit that clings to tooth surfaces and below the gum line. Left untreated, it leads to gum inflammation, periodontal disease, tooth decay, and persistent bad breath. Professional teeth cleaning at our dental clinic in Ajman removes these deposits safely and effectively, restoring your oral health and protecting you from more serious and costly treatments down the road.

Scaling, Polishing, and Deep Cleaning Explained

Understanding the different types of dental cleaning helps you know exactly what to expect when you visit us.

Scaling

Scaling is the foundation of every professional cleaning appointment. Using advanced ultrasonic instruments combined with precision hand scalers, our dental team removes plaque and tartar from all tooth surfaces, including the hard-to-reach areas between teeth and along the gum line. Ultrasonic scaling uses gentle vibrations and a fine water mist to break apart calculus quickly and comfortably. This process is essential for preventing gingivitis and stopping early gum disease before it progresses.

Polishing

After scaling, we polish your teeth using a slow-speed handpiece fitted with a soft rubber cup and professional prophylaxis paste. Polishing smooths the enamel surface, removes superficial stains caused by tea, coffee, and tobacco, and makes it harder for new plaque to adhere. Many of our patients in Ajman notice an immediate improvement in the brightness of their smile after a single scaling and polishing session.

Deep Cleaning (Scaling and Root Planing)

For patients with moderate to advanced gum disease, standard scaling and polishing may not be enough. Deep cleaning, also known as scaling and root planing, involves cleaning below the gum line to remove bacterial deposits from the root surfaces. This treatment smooths the roots to help gums reattach to the teeth, reduce pocket depth, and halt the progression of periodontal disease. Dr. Manoj Mathew will assess your gums carefully and recommend deep cleaning only when it is clinically necessary.

What to Expect During Your Cleaning Visit

A typical teeth cleaning appointment at Rashid Medical Centre takes between 30 and 45 minutes. Here is what happens step by step:

  1. Oral examination: Your dentist examines your teeth and gums for signs of decay, inflammation, or other concerns. Digital X-rays may be taken if needed.
  2. Ultrasonic scaling: Hardened plaque and tartar are removed from above and below the gum line using ultrasonic instruments.
  3. Hand scaling: Precision hand scalers are used to clean any remaining deposits in tight or curved areas.
  4. Polishing: Teeth are polished with prophylaxis paste to remove surface stains and smooth the enamel.
  5. Fluoride application: A fluoride treatment may be applied to strengthen enamel and provide extra cavity protection.
  6. Personalised advice: Your dentist discusses findings and provides tailored tips on brushing technique, flossing, and diet.

Comfortable and gentle: Many patients tell us they were nervous before their first cleaning but were pleasantly surprised by how comfortable the experience was. If you have sensitive teeth or dental anxiety, let us know and we will take extra care to ensure you feel at ease throughout the procedure.

How Often Should You Get Your Teeth Cleaned?

The standard recommendation is a professional dental cleaning every six months. This interval allows your dentist to catch early signs of decay or gum disease before they become serious. However, some patients benefit from more frequent cleanings:

  • Every 3 to 4 months if you have a history of gum disease or periodontal pockets deeper than 4mm
  • Every 3 to 4 months if you have diabetes, as the condition increases susceptibility to gum infections
  • Every 4 months if you are a smoker, since tobacco use accelerates plaque and tartar buildup
  • Every 6 months for most healthy adults and children over the age of three

During your visit to our dental clinic in Ajman, Dr. Manoj Mathew will evaluate your oral health and recommend the cleaning schedule that is right for you.

Preventing Gum Disease and Cavities

Professional teeth cleaning is one of the most effective ways to prevent the two most common oral health problems: gum disease and dental cavities. Here is how regular cleaning protects you:

Prevents gingivitis Removes plaque before it inflames your gums
Stops tooth decay Clears bacteria that produce enamel-eroding acids
Eliminates bad breath Targets odour-causing bacteria and tartar
Preserves bone health Prevents bone loss caused by advanced gum disease

Studies show that patients who maintain regular dental cleaning appointments are significantly less likely to need fillings, root canals, or tooth extractions later in life. Think of it as a small investment of time and money that saves you from far greater expense and discomfort in the future.
