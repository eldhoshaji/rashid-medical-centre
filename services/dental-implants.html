What Are Dental Implants?

A dental implant is a small titanium post that is surgically placed into the jawbone to replace the root of a missing tooth. Once the implant has healed and fused with the bone through a natural process called osseointegration, a custom-made dental crown is attached on top. The result is a replacement tooth that is virtually indistinguishable from your natural teeth.

Titanium is biocompatible, meaning the body accepts it without rejection. This is what allows the implant to bond permanently with the jawbone, creating a foundation that is just as strong and stable as a natural tooth root. Whether you are missing a single tooth, several teeth, or a full arch, dental implants can be tailored to your situation.

The Dental Implant Procedure: Step by Step

Understanding the process helps reduce anxiety and set realistic expectations. At Rashid Medical Centre, every implant case is carefully planned and executed with precision. Here is what to expect:

1

Initial Consultation and Assessment

Your journey begins with a thorough examination. We take digital X-rays to assess your bone density, evaluate the condition of surrounding teeth, and discuss your goals. You will receive an honest assessment of whether implants are the right option for you, along with a clear cost estimate.

2

Treatment Planning

A personalised treatment plan is created based on your X-rays and clinical findings. If bone grafting is needed to strengthen the jaw, this will be discussed and scheduled. We plan the exact position and angle of the implant for optimal results.

3

Implant Placement Surgery

The titanium implant post is surgically placed into the jawbone under local anaesthesia. The procedure typically takes 30 to 60 minutes per implant. Most patients report minimal discomfort — far less than they anticipated. A temporary restoration may be placed over the implant site.

4

Healing and Osseointegration

Over the next 3 to 6 months, the implant gradually fuses with the jawbone. This is the most important phase — it is what gives the implant its exceptional strength and stability. During this period, you can go about your daily life normally.

5

Abutment and Crown Placement

Once healing is confirmed, an abutment (connector piece) is attached to the implant, and a custom-made porcelain crown is fabricated to match the colour, size, and shape of your natural teeth. The crown is then permanently secured. Your new tooth is complete.

6

Follow-Up and Maintenance

We schedule follow-up visits to ensure the implant is integrating well and your bite is comfortable. After that, you simply care for your implant the same way you care for your natural teeth — brushing, flossing, and regular check-ups.

Aftercare and Longevity of Dental Implants

One of the greatest advantages of dental implants is their longevity. Unlike dentures that need relining or bridges that may need replacing every 10 to 15 years, a well-maintained implant can last a lifetime. The key to long-lasting results is straightforward:

  • Brush twice daily with a soft-bristled toothbrush, paying attention to the gum line around the implant
  • Floss daily — use interdental brushes or a water flosser to clean around the implant post
  • Avoid excessive force — do not use your implant tooth to open bottles or crack hard shells
  • Quit smoking — smoking significantly increases the risk of implant failure and gum disease
  • Attend regular check-ups — visit Rashid Medical Centre every 6 months for professional cleaning and implant monitoring

While the titanium post is designed to last permanently, the porcelain crown on top may eventually show wear after 15 to 20 years. Replacing the crown is a simple, non-surgical procedure that restores the implant to like-new condition.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do dental implants last?
With proper care and regular dental check-ups, dental implants can last 20 years or more — many patients enjoy them for a lifetime. The titanium post fuses permanently with the jawbone, and the crown on top can be replaced if it ever wears down. Maintaining good oral hygiene and attending routine visits at our dental clinic in Ajman are the best ways to ensure your implant lasts as long as possible.
Is dental implant surgery painful?
The procedure is performed under local anaesthesia, so you will not feel pain during surgery. Most patients report only mild discomfort for a few days afterwards, which is easily managed with prescribed pain relief. Many patients tell us the experience was much easier than they expected. Am I a good candidate for dental implants?
Most adults with good general health are suitable candidates for dental implants. You need adequate jawbone density to support the implant — if bone has been lost, a bone graft may be recommended first. Conditions such as uncontrolled diabetes or heavy smoking may affect healing.
