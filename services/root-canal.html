What Is a Root Canal and When Do You Need One?

A root canal, also known as endodontic therapy, is a dental procedure designed to save a tooth that has become severely infected or damaged. Deep inside every tooth lies a soft tissue called the dental pulp, which contains nerves, blood vessels, and connective tissue. When bacteria penetrate the outer layers of a tooth through deep decay, cracks, or trauma, they can infect the pulp and cause intense pain, swelling, and even abscess formation.

During root canal treatment, the infected pulp is carefully removed, the interior canal system is thoroughly cleaned and disinfected, and the tooth is sealed with a biocompatible filling material. A dental crown is then placed over the treated tooth to restore its full strength and natural appearance. The goal is straightforward: eliminate the infection while preserving your natural tooth so you can avoid extraction and the need for costly replacements like implants or bridges.

You may need a root canal if you experience any of the following symptoms:

  • Severe, persistent toothache that worsens at night
  • Prolonged sensitivity to hot or cold food and drinks
  • Darkening or discolouration of a tooth
  • Swelling and tenderness in the surrounding gums
  • A recurring pimple or boil on the gum near the affected tooth
  • Pain when biting or chewing

If you notice any of these warning signs, it is important to visit a qualified dentist promptly. Delaying treatment can allow the infection to spread to neighbouring teeth or the jawbone, leading to more serious complications.

Our Step-by-Step Process

  1. Thorough Diagnosis: We begin with a comprehensive clinical examination and digital X-rays to assess the extent of infection and map the root canal anatomy with precision.
  2. Comfortable Anaesthesia: Advanced local anaesthesia is administered to completely numb the treatment area. You will feel no pain during the procedure.
  3. Pulp Removal & Cleaning: Using specialised instruments, the infected pulp tissue is carefully removed. The canals are cleaned, shaped, and irrigated with antimicrobial solutions to eliminate all bacteria.
  4. Filling & Sealing: The cleaned canals are filled with a biocompatible material called gutta-percha and sealed to prevent reinfection.
  5. Crown Placement: A custom-fitted dental crown is placed over the tooth to restore its structure, function, and appearance.

Recovery and Aftercare

Recovery after a root canal is typically smooth and straightforward. Most patients can return to their daily activities the same day. Here are a few aftercare guidelines to ensure the best healing:

  • Avoid chewing on the treated side until the permanent crown is placed.
  • Take any prescribed pain relievers or antibiotics as directed.
  • Maintain excellent oral hygiene by brushing twice daily and flossing.
  • Attend your follow-up appointment within one to two weeks for crown placement.
  • Avoid very hard or sticky foods for a few days after the procedure.

Mild soreness or sensitivity around the treated tooth is perfectly normal for a few days and can be managed with over-the-counter pain medication. If you experience severe pain, persistent swelling, or fever, contact our clinic immediately.
