Preventive Treatments and Dental Sealants

Prevention is the cornerstone of paediatric dental care. At our Ajman clinic, we focus on stopping dental problems before they start, rather than treating them after they develop. Our preventive services for children include:

  • Dental sealants -- thin, protective coatings applied to the grooves and pits of back teeth (molars) where food particles and bacteria tend to collect. Sealants create a smooth barrier that is easy to clean and can reduce the risk of cavities by up to 80%.
  • Fluoride treatments -- professional-strength fluoride applications that strengthen tooth enamel and make teeth more resistant to acid attacks from plaque bacteria and sugars.
  • Thorough cleanings -- gentle yet effective professional cleanings to remove plaque and tartar build-up that regular brushing may miss, especially in hard-to-reach areas of a child's smaller mouth.
  • Dietary and hygiene guidance -- personalised advice for parents on nutrition, brushing techniques, and habits that support strong, healthy teeth throughout childhood.

These preventive measures are quick, painless, and extremely cost-effective compared to treating cavities and other dental issues later on. Our kids dentist in Ajman recommends sealants as soon as the first permanent molars appear, typically around age six, to give your child the best possible protection during cavity-prone years.

Early Orthodontic Assessments

The alignment of your child's teeth and jaw plays a crucial role in their long-term oral health, speech development, and self-confidence. At Rashid Medical Centre, we recommend that every child receive an initial orthodontic screening by age seven. At this stage, a mix of baby teeth and permanent teeth are present, giving our dentists a clear picture of how the bite is developing.

Early assessment allows us to identify potential issues such as crowding, crossbites, overbites, underbites, and spacing problems well before they become complex. In many cases, early intervention -- sometimes called interceptive orthodontics -- can guide jaw growth and tooth eruption, reducing or even eliminating the need for extensive treatment later in adolescence.

Did you know? The American Association of Orthodontists recommends that children receive their first orthodontic evaluation by age 7. Early detection gives your child the best chance at a healthier, straighter smile with less invasive treatment.

When to Bring Your Child for Their First Visit

As a general guideline, your child should visit the dentist by their first birthday or within six months of their first tooth appearing, whichever comes first. This initial visit is gentle and brief -- it allows our pediatric dentist in Ajman to check that teeth are developing properly, assess the health of gums and soft tissues, and discuss feeding habits with you.

After the first visit, we recommend check-ups every six months to monitor growth, catch any early signs of decay, and reinforce good oral hygiene habits. Regular visits also help your child build a trusting relationship with our dental team, which makes future appointments easier and more comfortable for everyone.

Tips for Parents: Building Healthy Dental Habits at Home

The foundation of a lifetime of healthy smiles begins at home. Here are practical tips our dentists share with every parent who visits our dental clinic in Ajman:

  • Start early: Begin cleaning your baby's gums with a soft, damp cloth even before the first tooth appears. Once teeth erupt, switch to a small, soft-bristled toothbrush with a rice-grain-sized smear of fluoride toothpaste.
  • Supervise brushing: Children under age eight generally lack the manual dexterity to brush effectively on their own. Supervise and assist with brushing morning and night until they can tie their own shoelaces -- a good indicator of readiness.
  • Limit sugary snacks and drinks: Frequent exposure to sugar is the number one cause of cavities in children. Encourage water instead of juice or fizzy drinks, and offer healthy snacks like cheese, vegetables, and nuts.
  • Lead by example: Children mirror their parents' behaviour. Let your child see you brushing and flossing regularly and talk positively about dental visits.
  • Use positive language: When discussing the dentist, avoid words like "pain," "needle," or "drill." Instead, frame visits as a fun adventure to keep teeth strong and sparkly.

Frequently Asked Questions

At what age should my child first visit the dentist? +

The American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry recommends that children visit the dentist by their first birthday or within six months after the first tooth erupts. At Rashid Medical Centre in Ajman, we welcome babies and toddlers for their very first dental check-up so we can monitor development and catch any concerns early.

Are dental sealants safe for children? +

Yes, dental sealants are completely safe and highly effective. They are thin, protective coatings applied to the chewing surfaces of back teeth where cavities are most likely to form. Sealants can reduce the risk of decay in molars by up to 80%. Our dentists apply sealants quickly and painlessly -- the procedure takes just a few minutes per tooth.

How can I prepare my child for their first dental visit? +

Keep the conversation positive and simple. Read children's books about visiting the dentist, avoid using words like "pain" or "needle," and let them know the dentist is a friendly helper who keeps teeth strong. At our Ajman dental clinic, we create a welcoming, fun environment so your child feels comfortable from the moment they walk in.

Does Rashid Medical Centre offer early orthodontic assessments for children? +

Yes. We recommend an orthodontic screening around age 7, when permanent teeth begin to emerge. Early assessment allows our doctors to identify issues such as crowding, crossbites, or jaw alignment problems and plan timely intervention if needed.
